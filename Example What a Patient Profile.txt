A Patient Profile is a subject-level report that presents all relevant clinical trial data for an individual patient in a structured format.
It usually includes demographic information, medical history, adverse events, concomitant medications, laboratory results, vital signs,
and other key study data. Patient profiles are mainly used for medical review and safety evaluation."

Key points you can mention

One report per subject

Used by medical monitors and clinicians

Combines data from multiple domains (DM, AE, CM, LB, VS, etc.)

Usually created using PROC REPORT or PROC TEMPLATE


2️⃣ Subject Listing

Interview answer:

"Subject listing is a tabular report that displays clinical data for multiple subjects in a structured listing format.
Each row represents an observation and each column represents a variable. Subject listings help reviewers examine study data such as adverse events,
lab results, or demographics across subjects."

Example domains

AE listing

Lab listing

Demographic listing

Concomitant medication listing

Tools used

PROC REPORT

PROC PRINT

3️⃣ Difference: Patient Profile vs Subject Listing (VERY important)

Interviewers LOVE this question.

🆚 Patient Profile vs Subject Listing

| Patient Profile          | Subject Listing        |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------- |
| One subject              | Many subjects          |
| Complete story           | Tabular data           |
| Multiple sections        | Usually one domain     |
| Used by medical reviewer | Used by DM / reviewers |
| Chronological            | Row-based              |

🍋 Lemon version

Patient profile = full movie of one patient 🎬

Subject listing = Excel sheet of many patients 📊

4️⃣ One-line interview answers (memorize)
🔹 Patient Profile

“A patient profile is a subject-level, chronological summary combining data across domains to support medical review.”

🔹 Subject Listing

“A subject listing is a tabular display of clinical data across multiple subjects used for data review and quality checks.”

5️⃣ Bonus line (sounds senior 👌)

“Patient profiles are designed to help reviewers quickly identify safety concerns, trends, and inconsistencies at the subject level.”

“In patient profiles, I create a subject-level summary by combining data from multiple domains into a chronological, reviewer-friendly format. I ensure data consistency, correct sequencing of events, and highlight important safety and protocol-related information to support medical review.”

“I pull data from domains like demographics, medical history, adverse events, concomitant medications, labs, vitals, and efficacy assessments. I align dates and times, sort events chronologically, and format the output so reviewers can easily understand the subject’s study journey.”

I check for missing data, date inconsistencies, overlap between adverse events and medications, abnormal lab values, protocol deviations, and overall safety trends at the subject level.”

In patient profiles, I show the complete story of one patient in one report and make sure everything makes sense and follows the protocol

Patient profiles help medical reviewers quickly assess subject safety, data quality, and compliance without going through multiple datasets.


3️⃣ e-Narratives

Interview answer:

"e-Narratives are electronic narratives that summarize important clinical events for a patient, usually serious adverse events or deaths. They combine clinical data from multiple datasets to provide a detailed medical story for regulatory review."

Typical contents

Patient demographics

Medical history

Adverse event details

Concomitant medications

Lab results

Outcome

Used for

Safety review

Regulatory submissions (FDA / regulatory authorities)
