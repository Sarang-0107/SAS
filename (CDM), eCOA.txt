In Clinical Data Management (CDM), the full form of eCOA is:
eCOA = electronic Clinical Outcome Assessment
What it refers to
eCOA is a digital method of capturing clinical outcome data directly from patients, clinicians, or caregivers. It replaces traditional paper-based assessments.
Types of assessments included under eCOA
eCOA can capture different types of outcome assessments:
ePRO – electronic Patient-Reported Outcomes
eClinRO – electronic Clinician-Reported Outcomes
eObsRO – electronic Observer-Reported Outcomes
ePerfO – electronic Performance Outcomes
Why it is used in CDM
Improves data quality and reduces transcription errors
Ensures real-time data capture
Enhances regulatory compliance
Improves patient engagement

The 4 Types of eCOA (Simple + Practical)
1️⃣ ePRO – electronic Patient-Reported Outcomes
🧠 Who reports?

👉 Patient

📝 What kind of data?

Symptoms

Pain

Quality of life

Daily diaries

📱 Example

“Rate your pain from 0–10”

“Did you have nausea today?”

📌 Key point

No clinician interpretation — patient speaks for themselves

🎤 Interview line

ePRO captures symptoms and experiences directly from the patient without clinician interpretation.

2️⃣ eClinRO – electronic Clinician-Reported Outcomes
🧠 Who reports?

👉 Clinician / Investigator

📝 What kind of data?

Disease severity

Tumor response

Clinical scales

🏥 Example

ECOG performance status

Tumor response per RECIST

📌 Key point

Based on clinical judgment and examination

🎤 Interview line

eClinRO data is entered by clinicians based on their professional assessment.

3️⃣ eObsRO – electronic Observer-Reported Outcomes
🧠 Who reports?

👉 Observer / Caregiver

📝 What kind of data?

Observable behavior

Functional status

👶 Example

Parent reporting child’s sleep

Caregiver reporting dementia behavior

📌 Key point

Observer reports what they see, not opinions

🎤 Interview line

eObsRO is used when patients cannot reliably self-report, such as in pediatric or cognitively impaired populations.

4️⃣ ePerfO – electronic Performance Outcomes
🧠 Who reports?

👉 Patient performs a task
👉 System records result

📝 What kind of data?

Objective performance measures

🚶 Example

6-minute walk test

Memory or reaction-time test

📌 Key point

Measures what patient can do, not what they feel

🎤 Interview line

ePerfO assesses patient performance through standardized tasks with objective scoring.

One-Table Summary (Memorize This)
| Type    | Who              | Example        | Key Risk                |
| ------- | ---------------- | -------------- | ----------------------- |
| ePRO    | Patient          | Pain score     | Missing entries         |
| eClinRO | Clinician        | Tumor response | Inter-rater variability |
| eObsRO  | Caregiver        | Child behavior | Subjectivity            |
| ePerfO  | Patient + system | Walk test      | Device variability      |

Super-Easy Memory Trick 🧠

PRO → Patient Reports Own feelings

ClinRO → Clinician Reports

ObsRO → Observer Reports

PerfO → Performance is Measured

One Killer Line (Use Anywhere)

ePRO, eClinRO, eObsRO, and ePerfO are different eCOA data types distinguished by who reports the outcome and how it is measured.

eCOA captures outcome assessments, EDC captures clinical CRF data, and IRT manages randomization and drug supply logistics.

eCOA is an electronic system used to capture clinical outcome assessments directly from patients, clinicians, or caregivers using digital devices. It improves data quality and timeliness and integrates with EDC. eCOA is not reconciliation; reconciliation is a downstream process to compare and align data across systems.

Mapping eCOA Types to SDTM Domains (QS vs CO)
First: The Golden Rule 🏆

SDTM domain is decided by the nature of the data, NOT by who enters it.

Keep this line in your head.

1️⃣ QS – Questionnaire Domain (MOST eCOA data)
When do we use QS?

Use QS when:

Data comes from a questionnaire, scale, or instrument

Responses are scores, ratings, yes/no

Fixed questions with defined answers

📌 This covers most eCOA

eCOA → QS Mapping

| eCOA Type   | Typical SDTM Domain | Why                               |
| ----------- | ------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **ePRO**    | QS                  | Patient answers questions         |
| **eClinRO** | QS                  | Clinician completes scale         |
| **eObsRO**  | QS                  | Caregiver completes questionnaire |
| **ePerfO**  | QS (usually)        | Task score recorded               |


Examples (QS)

| Assessment          | Domain | Reason                  |
| ------------------- | ------ | ----------------------- |
| Pain scale (0–10)   | QS     | Questionnaire           |
| QoL (EQ-5D)         | QS     | Instrument-based        |
| ECOG score          | QS     | Rating scale            |
| 6-minute walk score | QS     | Standardized assessment |

2️⃣ CO – Clinical Outcome Domain (LESS COMMON)
When do we use CO?

Use CO when:

Outcome is qualitative clinical observation

Not a formal questionnaire

Free-text or categorical clinical outcome

📌 CO is not common for eCOA but does exist


What is ECOG?

ECOG = Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group Performance Status

It measures a patient’s functional status — basically:

How well can the patient perform daily activities?

ECOG Scale (0–5)
Score	Meaning
0	Fully active
1	Restricted in strenuous activity
2	Ambulatory, unable to work
3	Limited self-care
4	Completely disabled
5	Dead

📌 Lower score = better condition

Is ECOG eCOA?

👉 Yes, it can be collected as eCOA
More specifically:

Type: eClinRO

Who enters it: Investigator / clinician

Tool: Paper or electronic (eCOA system like Veeva)

ECOG → SDTM Mapping (Very Important 🔥)
❓ QS or CO?

✅ QS domain

Why?

ECOG is a standardized rating scale

Fixed response values (0–5)

Not a free-text outcome

Example SDTM Mapping (QS)
Variable	Value
QSCAT	ECOG PERFORMANCE STATUS
QSTEST	ECOG Score
QSTESTCD	ECOG
QSORRES	2
QSSTRESC	2
QSVISIT	Week 4

📌 Even though clinician enters it → still QS

Interview Trap (Watch Out ⚠️)

❌ “ECOG is a clinical outcome so it goes to CO”
✅ Wrong

Correct response:

ECOG is a clinician-reported outcome collected using a standardized scale, so it is mapped to the QS domain.

One-Line Interview Answer (Memorize This)

ECOG is a clinician-reported performance status scale, often collected via eCOA, and is mapped to the QS domain in SDTM.

Quick Memory Hook 🧠

ECOG = Scale → QS

Entered by clinician → eClinRO

Oncology staple → Very common

eCOA → CO Mapping (Edge Cases)

| Data Type                                        | Domain | Why                 |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ------ | ------------------- |
| Clinician observation (e.g., “Disease improved”) | CO     | Clinical outcome    |
| Observer notes (non-scored)                      | CO     | Observation         |
| Functional outcome without scale                 | CO     | Outcome description |

3️⃣ Decision Tree (Think This Way in Interview)

Ask yourself:

Is this from a defined questionnaire or scale?

Yes → QS

Is it a descriptive clinical outcome?

Yes → CO

📌 Who entered it (patient/clinician) is secondary.

4️⃣ Interview Trap Question ⚠️

❓ “eClinRO always goes to CO, right?”

❌ Wrong

✅ Correct answer:

eClinRO data usually maps to QS when it comes from a standardized clinical scale. CO is used only when the data represents a descriptive clinical outcome rather than a questionnaire-based assessment.

5️⃣ One-Table Cheat Sheet (Save This 🧠)

| Scenario                     | Domain |
| ---------------------------- | ------ |
| Questionnaire / scale        | QS     |
| Rating score                 | QS     |
| Yes/No question              | QS     |
| Structured assessment        | QS     |
| Descriptive clinical outcome | CO     |

6️⃣ One-Line Interview Answer (Perfect 💎)

Most eCOA data maps to the QS domain because it is collected through standardized questionnaires or scales. The CO domain is used only for descriptive clinical outcomes that are not questionnaire-based.

Final Memory Hook 🔐

If it looks like a question, it’s QS.
If it looks like a conclusion, it’s CO.
